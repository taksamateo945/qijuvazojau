Minnie Driver - Bio, Career, Age, Net Worth, Height, Facts

Minnie Driver is a popular English actress and singer. Minnie Driver rose to fame with her break-out role in 1995’s Circle of Friends. She has also lent her voice to a variety of animated films and series.

Early Life and Childhood

Minnie Driver was born on January 31, 1970, in the beautiful city of London, United Kingdom. Her age is 53 years as of 2023 and her full name is Amelia Fiona Jessica Driver.

Similarly, she is British and American by nationality and Christian by religion. Moreover, she belongs to the English and Scottish ethnicity and her zodiac sign is Aquarius.

She was born to a director of London United Investments Charles Ronald Driver (father) and a fabric designer and former model Gaynor Churchward (mother). Likewise, her older sister’s name is Kate and she is a film producer and manager by profession.

Her parents were not married as her father was married to another woman throughout his relationship with her mother. Later, her parents broke off their relationship when she was six years old.

Moreover, she also has one older half-sister, Susan Driver, from her father’s previous marriage, and two younger half-brothers, Charlie Driver, and Ed Churchward.

Career and Professional Life

Moving on to her career, she made her television debut in a Right Guard deodorant commercial in 1991. Similarly, she also made her stage debut, as a jazz vocalist and guitarist in 1991. She appeared in an episode of Maigret with Michael Gambon and Michael Sheen in 1993.

Likewise, she also appeared on British television in The Day Today with comedian Steve Coogan. Moreover, she rose to prominence after starring opposite Chris O’Donnell in the 1995 film Circle of Friends. In addition, she also did minor roles in movies like GoldenEye, Sleepers, Big Night, and Grosse Pointe Blank.

Furthermore, she was also considered for the role of Rose DeWitt Butaker in the 1997 film Titanic, but director James Cameron felt she was too old, so Kate Winslet was cast instead. Besides that, she also appeared in Sandra Goldbacher’s film The Governess in 1998.

That same year, she co-starred in the action thriller Hard Rain with Christian Slater and Morgan Freeman. She has also voiced Jane in Disney’s 1999 Tarzan remake and Lady Eboshi in the 1999 English-dubbed release of the Japanese film Princess Mononoke.

However, she returned to television in March 2007 with the FX Network show The Riches. Later, she began filming for The Crash in October 2013, and it was released on January 13, 2017.

In 2021, she launched Minnie Questions with Minnie Driver, a podcast in which she asks guests a series of seven questions inspired by Proust’s Questionnaire.
